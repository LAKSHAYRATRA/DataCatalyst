WHY YOU AND YOUR BUDDY CAN'T HEAR EACH OTHER ON A HINDI CALL
==============================================================

There are two things that could be causing this. One of them is a
code problem, the other is a network problem. Read both and see
which one matches what you're actually experiencing.


------------------------------------------------------------------
REASON 1 — YOU MIGHT NOT EVEN BE GETTING CONNECTED
------------------------------------------------------------------

The website recently added a new rule for who is allowed to make
calls. To join the call queue, your Hindi approval has to be
marked as a "call" application specifically.

Where the rule lives: voicechat-backend/src/index.js:743
Where the setting lives: voicechat-backend/src/models/User.js:60

Think of it like a movie theater that just added a new rule:
"Only tickets stamped 'evening show' are allowed in tonight."
If your Hindi ticket was printed before that rule existed, or
was stamped 'phrase' instead of 'call', the doorman turns you
away — even though it says "Hindi approved" right on the ticket.

What you would see:
- A popup that says "You are not approved to call in Hindi yet.
  Would you like to apply now?"
- Or you just sit on "finding match" forever while your buddy
  seems to also be stuck.

You would NOT see the topic selection screen or the countdown.

How to fix it:
- Both people need a Hindi application where the type is 'call'.
- Either re-apply through the "Call Application" tab on the
  language apply page (voicechat-frontend/src/pages/LanguageApply.jsx:239)
  and get an admin to approve it,
- Or go directly into Mongo and set the field:

    db.users.find({ "languageApplications.languageCode": "hindi" },
                  { username: 1, "languageApplications.$": 1 })

    // then for each user missing the flag:
    $set: { "languageApplications.$.applicationType": "call" }


------------------------------------------------------------------
REASON 2 — YOU'RE CONNECTED, BUT THE AUDIO CAN'T GET THROUGH
------------------------------------------------------------------

If you actually reach the topic-selection screen and the countdown,
and then it just goes silent — the code is fine, the connection
is stuck at the network level.

Where the setup lives: voicechat-frontend/src/pages/Call.jsx:20-29

Think of it like two people trying to call each other's phones,
but both phones are inside locked apartment buildings. You know
each other's addresses (that's what STUN does — it exchanges
addresses), but neither of you can actually get past the other's
front door (the firewall / NAT). So no sound gets through.

The site currently only has STUN and no TURN. That means:

- Same Wi-Fi, same house, same LAN? Works. No locked doors between you.
- Two browsers on the same laptop? Definitely works — that's why
  you heard yourself echo.
- Two different networks across the internet? Often fails.
  Mobile data, office Wi-Fi, hotels, some home ISPs — the doors
  are locked and STUN alone can't open them.

How to confirm this is what's happening:
- Open DevTools during the call.
- Look for "ICE Connection Failed" in the console
  (logged from voicechat-frontend/src/pages/Call.jsx:417).
- Or open chrome://webrtc-internals and watch the ICE candidate
  pairs — they'll stay in "checking" or "failed" and never
  reach "succeeded".

How to fix it:
Add a TURN server. TURN is basically a middleman meeting spot —
instead of trying to force open each other's front doors, both
of you walk to a coffee shop in the middle and hand your voice
packets to a barista who passes them along. This works because
both of you are the ones reaching OUT (which locked doors don't
block), instead of trying to be reached IN.

Set this in voicechat-frontend/.env
(NOT the backend .env — it's a frontend variable because it's
read via import.meta.env):

    VITE_ICE_SERVERS=[{"urls":"stun:stun.l.google.com:19302"},{"urls":"turn:YOUR_TURN_HOST:3478","username":"USER","credential":"PASS"}]

Options for the TURN server itself:
- Quick free test (dev only): Open Relay Project at
  metered.ca/tools/openrelay — gives working credentials in a
  minute. But their free servers are US-only, so India users
  will feel a walkie-talkie delay.
- Best for India users: rent a small $5/month VPS in Mumbai or
  Singapore and run coturn on it. Low delay, cheap.
- Best zero-hassle option: Twilio Network Traversal — pay per
  GB, has servers everywhere, low delay.

After you change the .env, REBUILD the frontend. Vite bakes env
variables in at build time, so a live-reload isn't enough.


------------------------------------------------------------------
IMPORTANT: TURN DOES NOT AFFECT YOUR RECORDINGS
------------------------------------------------------------------

If you were worried that adding TURN would mess up the two
separated speaker recordings (like the 19:30 vs 19:45 files) —
it won't.

Each person's recording is captured from their OWN microphone,
locally in their own browser, and mailed to the server over a
separate socket connection. TURN is only involved in the LIVE
conversation between the two of you. It never touches the
recording files.

The reason the two recordings have slightly different lengths
is a separate small timing issue in when each side starts and
stops recording (see startCallRecording in
voicechat-frontend/src/pages/Call.jsx:346), not a TURN problem.


------------------------------------------------------------------
QUICK CHECK — WHICH REASON IS YOURS?
------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Do you get a "would you like to apply" popup, or stay stuck
   on the "finding match" spinner?
   → Reason 1 (application type).

Q: Do you actually reach the topic-selection screen and the
   5-second countdown, and THEN it goes silent?
   → Reason 2 (no TURN server).

Q: Do two accounts on the same laptop echo fine, but a real
   buddy on a different network hears nothing?
   → Reason 2 (no TURN server). Confirmed.
